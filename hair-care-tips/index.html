Expert Hair Care Advice

Hair Care Tips from
Pittsburgh's Top Stylists

Professional advice to keep your hair healthy, vibrant, and beautiful between salon visits. From the expert team at Craft Collective Salon Group.

01

Use Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates strip color and natural oils from your hair. Switch to a sulfate-free formula, especially if you have color-treated hair. Your color will last significantly longer.

02

Deep Condition Weekly

A professional-grade conditioning mask once a week restores moisture, improves elasticity, and adds shine. Apply to damp hair, leave for 5 to 10 minutes, and rinse with cool water to seal the cuticle.

03

Always Use Heat Protection

Heat tools open the hair cuticle and accelerate color fading and dryness. Apply a thermal protectant to damp hair before blow drying and again to dry hair before flat ironing or curling.

04

Wash Less Frequently

Every shampoo strips a small amount of color and natural oils. Going from daily washing to every 2 to 3 days can dramatically extend your color life and improve hair health. Use dry shampoo on non-wash days.

05

Protect from UV Damage

Sun exposure fades hair color just like it fades fabric. Use a leave-in spray with UV filters during Pittsburgh's summer months. Hats help too, especially for blondes and highlighted hair.

06

Rinse with Cool Water

Hot water opens the cuticle and releases color molecules. Finishing your shower with a cool rinse seals the cuticle, locking in moisture and color. It makes a noticeable difference in shine.

07

Get Regular Trims

Trimming every 8 to 12 weeks prevents split ends from traveling up the hair shaft. Healthy ends make your color look better and your hair feel thicker. Ask your stylist for a maintenance schedule.

08

Sleep on Silk or Satin

Cotton pillowcases create friction that causes frizz and breakage. A silk or satin pillowcase reduces friction, preserves your style, and is especially important for clients with hair extensions.

09

Use Professional Products

Professional salon products use higher concentrations of active ingredients and gentler formulations than drugstore alternatives. The upfront cost is higher but you use less product and get better results.

10

Hydrate from the Inside

Hair health starts from within. Drinking enough water, eating protein-rich foods, and taking biotin or collagen supplements can improve hair strength, shine, and growth over time.

11

Avoid Chlorine and Hard Water

Pool chlorine and mineral-heavy water can alter your hair color and cause dryness. Wet your hair with clean water before swimming and use a chelating shampoo periodically to remove mineral buildup.

12

Ask Your Stylist

Your stylist knows your specific hair type, color, and condition better than any blog post can. At your next appointment at Craft Collective, ask for a personalized home care routine.

Seasonal Hair Care for Pittsburgh Clients

Pittsburgh's climate presents unique challenges for hair health. Cold, dry winters strip moisture from your hair. Humid summers cause frizz and can accelerate color fading. Here is how to adjust your routine by season.

Winter Hair Care

Increase your deep conditioning frequency during the cold months. Static and dryness from indoor heating are common. A leave-in conditioner or hair oil applied to the mid-lengths and ends can combat winter dryness. If you have color-treated hair, consider scheduling your touch-ups slightly closer together during winter since dry hair can appear more faded.

Summer Hair Care

UV protection becomes critical during Pittsburgh's sunny summer months. Use products with UV filters and consider a keratin treatment to combat humidity-related frizz. Rinse your hair after swimming and avoid pulling wet hair into tight ponytails, which can cause breakage.
