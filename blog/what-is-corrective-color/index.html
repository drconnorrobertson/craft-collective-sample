Corrective Color

What Is Corrective Color?
How We Fix Bad Color Jobs

March 20, 2026 · By Derek Piekarski · Craft Collective Salon Group

When Hair Color Goes Wrong

It happens more often than you might think. A color appointment at another salon leaves you with brassy orange tones, uneven patches, or a shade that is nothing like what you asked for. Box dye disasters, over-processed bleach jobs, and amateur highlight work are some of the most common issues we see at Craft Collective Salon Group. The good news is that corrective color can fix nearly any color problem, and our team has the training to do it right.

What Is Corrective Color?

Corrective color is a specialized hair color service designed to fix, repair, or rebuild hair color that has gone wrong. It is not a standard color appointment. Corrective color requires a deep understanding of color theory, hair chemistry, and the way different products interact with each other.

Our owner, Derek Piekarski, spent years training colorists for Wella Professionals and Aveda on exactly these types of complex color challenges. That expertise is now available to Pittsburgh clients who need their color corrected professionally and safely.

Common Color Problems We Fix

Brassy or Orange Tones

This is the most common issue, especially with blonde hair. When hair is lightened without proper toning, or when box dye fades unevenly, warm undertones can appear. Our colorists use professional-grade toners and glazes to neutralize brassiness and restore your desired shade.

Uneven Color or Banding

Banding happens when different sections of your hair have been processed to different levels over time, creating visible lines or stripes. Fixing banding requires careful sectioning and targeted application to even out the color from root to tip.

Over-Processed or Damaged Hair

If your hair has been over-bleached or chemically damaged, color correction starts with assessing the structural integrity of the hair. We may recommend a course of strengthening treatments before any additional color work to prevent breakage.

Box Dye Buildup

Box dyes deposit metallic salts and other compounds that can react unpredictably with professional color products. Removing or working through box dye buildup requires patience, expertise, and a multi-step approach that only a trained colorist should attempt.

The Corrective Color Process at Craft Collective

Every corrective color service begins with an extended consultation. We need to understand your complete color history: what products have been used, how recently, and what your end goal is. Depending on the severity of the issue, corrective color may take multiple sessions spaced weeks apart. We will never rush the process or compromise your hair health to get a faster result.

Pricing for corrective color is quoted during your consultation because every case is different. We charge based on time and materials, and we are transparent about what the process will involve before we begin.

How to Avoid Needing Color Correction

The best way to avoid corrective color is to start with a qualified colorist from the beginning. Our color services are designed to deliver consistent, predictable results using professional-grade Wella products. When you invest in expert color from the start, you save money and protect your hair in the long run.
